## Abstract

We present **amore**, an R package that consolidates the modern relational-event modelling (REM) and relational hyper-event (RHEM) workflow developed by the Wit group into a single, unit-tested, CRAN-targeted toolchain. amore closes the full analysis loop: event simulation (exact Gillespie and approximate tau-leap; `R/simulate_events.R`), case-control non-event sampling including the Lembo et al. (2025) time-shift scheme for global covariates (`R/compare_models_global.R`, `R/transform_recency.R`), a history-aware endogenous-statistics engine with a C++ fast path and an R fallback (`R/preprocess.R`, `src/compute_features_cpp.cpp`), a unified `rem()` fitter, and the Boschi & Wit (2025) martingale-residual goodness-of-fit family for the linear case-control fit (`R/gof.R`). Its expository spine is **one likelihood, three predictors**: a linear conditional logit and a pure-R neural MLP both maximize the same risk-set softmax conditional-logistic partial likelihood, and an `mgcv` smooth/time-varying backend fits the algebraically-related degenerate (case-1-control) logistic regression — all behind one front-end. Correctness rests on a cross-implementation parity oracle (simulator vs post-hoc engine to 1e-9) and 48 test files. The contribution is the integrated, reproducible artifact and its unifying frame, **not new statistics**; we cite DREAM (Filippi-Mazzola & Wit 2024, the same group's neural REM) and the neural temporal-point-process literature as prior art.
